# Tavern Rat Race

*A fiercely competitive, chaotic struggle through an ever-shifting maze. Scramble, fight, and claw your way to the finish — because in this race, only the most cunning rat wins.*

---

## Setup
1. Arrange 9 dungeon tiles in a 3×3 grid to form a maze.
2. Place one piece of cheese on each corner tile (4 total).
3. Each player chooses a rat token and places it at the start.
4. Designate one tile as the finish.

> Multiple rats may occupy the same space.

---

## Core Concepts

**Exploding Dice**
When you roll the maximum value on a die, roll it again and add the result. Keep rolling as long as you hit the maximum. There is no ceiling — a lucky rat can cross the entire maze in one move.
*Example: Rolling a 1d6, you roll a 6, then a 6 again, then a 3 — your total movement is 15.*

**Movement Die**
All rats begin with a 1d4 movement die. Each piece of cheese a rat eats upgrades its movement die by one size: 1d4 → 1d6 → 1d8 → 1d10 → 1d12. The movement die is always rolled as an exploding die.

**Random Events**
Your movement die also serves as your Random Event die. When you roll a Random Event, roll your movement die (exploding) and consult the table. Higher dice produce wilder, more powerful events — rewarding rats who've eaten cheese.

---

## Turn Sequence

Each round proceeds in the following order:

### 1. Movement
- All rats roll their movement die (exploding). Rats move in order from highest to lowest roll. Ties move simultaneously.
- A rat that reaches a cheese tile stops immediately, eats the cheese, and upgrades its movement die by one size. It forfeits any remaining movement.
- A rat that reaches the finish wins the game.
- If two or more rats reach the same cheese or the finish simultaneously, a **Fight** occurs.

### 2. Random Event — Leading Rat
After all rats have moved, the rat currently in the lead rolls a Random Event.

### 3. Maze Shift
The DM rolls 1d8, 1d12, and 1d4:
- **d8** — That numbered tile swaps places with the center tile.
- **d12** — That numbered tile is rotated clockwise. On a 10 or 11, place a new cheese on a random tile. On a **12**, all 9 tiles rotate, and a new piece of cheese is placed on a random non-center tile.
- **d4** — Determines how many times the indicated tile is rotated.

> If the d8 or d12 indicate an empty or centermost tile, the DM may reroll or skip at their discretion.

---

## Fight

When two or more rats occupy the same space and contest it:
1. All contesting rats roll a d20.
2. The **winner** claims the space, rolls a **Random Event**, and may move the loser back 1 space in any direction.
3. The **loser** is moved back 1 space (winner's choice of direction).
4. On a tie, the rats are deadlocked — they must **Fight again** at the start of the next round.

---

## Stun

A stunned rat skips its next movement step. It still rolls its Random Event if applicable. Stuns do not stack.

---

## Cheese

- When a rat eats a piece of cheese, a new piece is immediately placed on a random non-center, non-finish tile. This ensures cheese is always available and the upgrade path stays open for every rat.

---

## Random Events

Roll your movement die (exploding). Events escalate the higher you roll — more powerful and chaotic results reward rats with bigger dice.

| Roll | Event |
|:----:|:------|
| 1 | Move one opponent 1 space. |
| 2 | Stun one opponent. |
| 3 | Move one opponent 1d4 spaces. |
| 4 | Gain a Reroll to use whenever you choose. |
| 5 | Rotate 1 random tile. |
| 6 | Swap 1 random tile with the center tile. |
| 7 | All rats roll to Fight, winner gets a free cheese. |
| 8 | Choose any tile and rotate it. |
| 9 | All rats move 3 spaces toward the center. |
| 10 | Swap any tile with the center. |
| 11 | Compel any two rats to move into the same space and Fight. |
| 12 | Roll 1d12 twice on this table. |
| 13+ | Your rat ran too fast and hit a wall — it is Stunned. |

---

## Variants
- **Custom Mazes:** Use a larger grid with adjusted dice for tile rotation and movement to suit the number of players or desired play length.
- **Simplified Rotation:** All tile rotations are a single clockwise turn — remove the d4 from the Maze Shift.
- **Continuous Chaos:** After every rat moves, apply a Random Event using the same roll. All rats trigger Random Events each turn, not just the leader.
